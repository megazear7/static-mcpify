# The Big Mountain

## description

The Big Mountain is a legendary peak standing at 14,259 feet in the heart of the Colorado Rockies. Known to the Ute people as "Sky Father," it has been a prominent landmark for centuries.

The mountain offers:
- Over 30 miles of maintained hiking trails ranging from moderate to expert
- World-class rock climbing on the north face
- A historic summit shelter built in 1927
- Stunning alpine wildflower meadows in summer
- Backcountry skiing in winter months

The Big Mountain draws approximately 100,000 visitors annually and is managed by the National Forest Service. The standard summit route takes 6-8 hours round trip and rewards climbers with panoramic views spanning five states. The mountain is also home to a rare population of alpine ptarmigan and Rocky Mountain bighorn sheep.
